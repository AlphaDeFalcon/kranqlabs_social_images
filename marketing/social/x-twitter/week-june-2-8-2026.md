## Thu Jun 4 — How We Think About Building Software

**Tweet 1 (main):**
First question we ask on every project:

"Will this actually change a number that matters?"

Not "what features do you want?"
Not "which stack?"

Find the metric. Then build the system to move it. 🧵

**Tweet 2:**
Most software projects start with a feature list.
Ours start with a measurement plan.

What number needs to change?
How will we know it changed?
Who needs to see it?

**Tweet 3:**
This makes the first week slower.
It makes everything after that much faster.

Because when you know what you're measuring, you know what to build. And more importantly, what NOT to build.

**Tweet 4:**
The expensive part of software isn't writing code.
It's building things nobody needed.

Metric first. Always.

## Sat Jun 6 — Why Most SME Websites Don't Convert

**Tweet 1 (main):**
You can diagnose a non-converting website in 5 seconds:

• No clear offer above the fold
• 3 competing CTAs
• "Welcome to our website" as the headline

Your site isn't a brochure. It's a salesperson. Treat it like one. 🧵

**Tweet 2:**
Give your website ONE job.

Product page? Sell the product.
Service page? Book the call.
Blog post? Get the email.

One page, one job, one CTA. Every time.

**Tweet 3:**
The best website advice nobody follows:

Remove everything that doesn't help the visitor decide.

That slideshow hero? Gone.
The "latest news" sidebar? Gone.
The "Welcome to our website"? Never existed.

**Tweet 4:**
Your website should work as hard as your best salesperson.

If it doesn't, it's not a design problem. It's a strategy problem.

#CRO #WebDesign #SMEGrowth
